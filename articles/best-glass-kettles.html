Are You Drinking Microplastics?

By Sarah Miller, PhD | March 11, 2026

Most "glass" kettles still have a plastic lid or glue sealant at the bottom. When steam hits that plastic, it drips microplastics right back into your tea. We performed a Steam Condensation Analysis on 15 models to find the survivors.

🔬 The PureCook Testing Protocol

We boiled water 100 times in each kettle, installing a high-precision particle trap at the spout. We analyzed the condensation under a laser microscope to detect BPA, phthalates, and sub-micron plastic fibers.
